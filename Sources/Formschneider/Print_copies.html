The surviving copies of the print are found in the following libraries:

Bressanone, Biblioteca del Seminario Maggiore “Vincentinum”
  • Complete copy, bound with volumes I and III
  • No handwritten annotations other than in-house corrections

Glasgow, University Library, Sp. Coll. R.b. 36-38

  • Altus, Tenor, and Bassus partbooks; the Tenor is from a different set from the other two
  • Several handwritten annotations
Halle, Universitätsbibliothek, Pon Iie 298 (1/3)
  • Altus partbook
  • No handwritten annotations other than in-house corrections
  • Online copy
Hradec Kralové 1264 (olim II A 35)
  • Discant and Altus partbooks
  • The editors have not seen this copy yet
Kraków, Jagellonian Library, Mus. Ant. Pract. I 215 & 216 (1+2)
  • Complete copy, bound with volumes I and III
  • No handwritten annotations other than in-house corrections
London, British Library, K.3.c.2
  • Complete copy, bound with volumes I and III
  • No handwritten annotations other than in-house corrections
Munich, Bayerische Staatsbibliothek 4o Mus. Pr. 123
  • Complete copy, bound with volumes I and III
  • No handwritten annotations other than in-house corrections
  • Online copy
Munich, Universitätsbibliothek, 4o Liturg 73(W)
  • Complete copy, bound with volumes I and III
  • Custos corrected by hand in Sequence 9, verse 22, T, fol. F3r, staff 2, end
Paris, Bibliothèque Nationale, Rés. 561 (+bis)
  • Tenor partbook
  • Handwritten cue notes (small black breves with fermatas after the clef, showing pitch on which the preceding incipit ends)
    • Introit 11, T, fol. G1v, staff 2: d’
    • Introit 11 (verse), T, fol. G1v, staff 5: f’
Regensburg, Bischöfliche Zentralbibliothek, A.R. 193-195
  • Complete copy, bound with volumes I and III
  • Numerous handwritten annotations
Uppsala, Univ. Bibl., Vok-mus I tr. 245-248
  • Complete copy, bound with volumes I and III
  • Numerous handwritten annotations
Washington, Library of Congress, M1490.I81 case: fragments
  • 14 numbered fragments, of which 12 are from the bassus of Choralis II and one each are from the tenor of Choralis I and III. Bleed-through from other pages shows on many of them.
  • Apparently cut up from unsold copies for use in book bindings. In-house corrections were entered before the pages were cut.
